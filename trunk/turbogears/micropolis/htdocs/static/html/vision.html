Micropolis Eduverse Vision

Micropolis, the open source version of SimCity, is a fun, engaging game, that helps teach kids about science, language, mathematics, art and politics. Its goal is to fulfill SimCity's potential as a microworld for children's learning and exploration.

A web based version of Micropolis is the best way to quickly reach the largest audience, and an important step towards the long term goal of developing an open source, colaborative multi player, educationally oriented simulation gaming platform.

This project is about creating educational open source software. It's the culmination of years of research and development, that is now possible thanks to Electronic Arts making SimCity open source.

Don Hopkins has worked with the SimCity source code for 16 years, ported it to many platforms, redesigned the user interface several times, designed and implemented colaborative multi player support, cleaned up and refactored the code, and integrated it with various scripting languages,

He has written ambitious but realistic and successful proposals, petitioning Electronic Arts to "Develop Third Party Content Authoring Tools for The Sims" (which led to Transmogrifier and the thriving Sims player created content community), and to develop "Educational Multi Player SimCity for Linux" (which led to EA releasing Open Source SimCity under GPLv3 for the OLPC, enabling the Micropolis project).

His long term dream of developing Micropolis into an educational gaming platform draws heavily on the vision and experience of educators, researchers and designers including Seymore Papert, Hal Abelson, Alan Kay, Will Wright, Ben Shneiderman, and Mark Weiser.

The Micropolis project is building an exemplary open source game out of modular reusable components, that other people can learn from, build on top of, integrate with other languages and user interfaces, and use as a starting point for their own projects.

The open source, component oriented approach to software development will result in portable, cross platform, educational software that can run on Linux, Mac, Windows, embeded devices, OLPC XO-1 laptops, and other children's computers.

These first steps will get us closer to realizing Alan Kay's vision of using microworlds and scriptable agents to teach kids science, mathematics and programming:
